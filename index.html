🏛 The main event — Underground River
🌊 Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park
UNESCO World Heritage Site 1999 New 7 Wonders of Nature 8.2 km · longest navigable underground river
The cave stretches 24 km through limestone and exits directly into the South China Sea. You explore 1.5 km by paddle boat — massive chambers, stalactites, millions of bats, endemic swiftlets. The 45-min boat tour inside the cave genuinely can't be captured in photos.

Critical: Daily cap is 900 visitors. No permit, no entry — no exceptions. Permits take 3 days to process. Book a tour operator who handles the permit from India.

💡 Tips
Book tours from your AirbnbTour operators are everywhere in El Nido Town. No need to pre-book from India — just walk in on Day 1 morning (or ask your Airbnb host). Book 1 day ahead for the next day's tour.
Private tour is worth it₱7,000–8,000 for a private boat (just the two of them) vs ₱1,200 each shared. For 2 people the maths is close, and private means your own pace, fewer crowds at the lagoons. Ask operators on arrival.
Carry a dry bagBuy in El Nido Town for ₱300–400. Essential on the boats — everything gets wet. Your phone, wallet, camera need protection especially in June.
Go early at Big LagoonThe tour boats arrive in waves. Get to Big Lagoon first — by 10 AM it gets busy. Operators doing reverse-order tours avoid the crowd.
June = fewer touristsLow season means quieter beaches, lower Airbnb rates, more relaxed tours. Island hopping still happens — June is just the start of the wet season, not peak monsoon.
Tricycle to everything₱50–100 per trip around El Nido Town / Corong Corong. No Grab. Agree on the price before you get in. Your Airbnb host will have numbers for reliable drivers.

🎒 Packing — what actually matters
🌊 Water & beach essentials
Rash guard / swim shirt
Long-sleeve rash guard — sun protection on island hopping is critical. 6 hrs on open water in June sun will burn. Lighter than sunscreen reapplication.
Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+)
Non-negotiable. Philippine parks increasingly enforce reef-safe only. Bring from India — expensive locally and hard to find the right kind.
Snorkel mask + fins (optional)
Tours provide basic snorkels but quality varies. If either of you has a prescription or comfort preferences, bring your own. Fins are usually provided.
Dry bag (10–20L)
Essential for island hopping — boat spray, lagoon entries, and pump boat crossings will wet everything. One good dry bag protects phones, passports, cash.
Water shoes / reef sandals
Lagoon entries, rocky shores, reef walks. Havaianas-style flip flops won't cut it in El Nido and Coron. Water shoes or Tevas are ideal.
2–3 swimsuits each
Washing in the sink every night gets old fast when you're island hopping 3 days in a row. Dry quickly in tropical heat but pack enough to rotate.
🎽 Clothes & nightlife
Smart casual for Manila nightlife
The Palace (Xylo/The Island) enforces smart casual — no slippers, no sleeveless shirts for men. One collared shirt + chinos at minimum. Pooja: no beachwear inside.
Light breathable clothing (4–5 outfits)
June in the Philippines is hot, humid, and occasionally rainy. Linen or moisture-wicking fabrics. Dark colours hide sweat. Pack light — you'll be island-hopping with a day bag most of the time.
1 compact rain jacket / packable poncho
June = wet season start. Short afternoon showers are common. A packable jacket doubles as windcheater on ferry decks. Don't bring an umbrella — useless in island wind.
Comfortable walking shoes + sandals
Manila (Intramuros cobblestones), Mt. Tapyas (723 steps), Cebu downtown. One pair of trainers or comfortable sneakers. Reef sandals can double as evening casual in El Nido.
🔋 Tech & gadgets
Universal travel adapter (Philippines: US Type A/B)
Philippines uses US-style flat 2-pin plugs. Most Indian devices (Type D/M) won't fit without an adapter. Grab a universal adapter — hotels in El Nido and Coron may not have spares.
Power bank (20,000 mAh)
Island hopping days are 8h+ with no charging. Coron guesthouses can be rustic. A high-capacity power bank keeps phones alive through long ferry days and late nights out.
Waterproof phone case / pouch
For the Big Lagoon kayak, Twin Lagoon swim-through, Barracuda cliff jump, and any pump boat crossing in rain. A cheap waterproof lanyard pouch (₹300 on Amazon) will do the job.
Action camera or GoPro (optional)
El Nido lagoons, Coron wrecks, Barracuda thermocline. If either of you has one, bring it — you'll regret not having underwater shots. Tours don't offer photographer services in most stops.
💊 Health & safety
Motion sickness tablets
Fast ferries (El Nido → Coron, 4–5h) can be rough in June swell. Island hopping pump boats too. Take Avomine or Stugeron 30 min before any sea crossing if prone.
Basic medical kit
Paracetamol, ORS sachets, antiseptic cream, band-aids, antacid, anti-diarrhoeal (Imodium). El Nido has no hospital — nearest is in Puerto Princesa. A well-stocked kit is not optional here.
Mosquito repellent (DEET 30%+)
Evening in El Nido and Coron Town has mosquitoes, especially after rain. Dengue is present in the Philippines. Long sleeves + repellent after sunset. Odomos or Repel 100 from India.
After-sun / aloe vera gel
You will get some sun regardless of how much you apply. Aloe vera gel takes up almost no space and provides significant relief after back-to-back days on the water.
